# Datasets are protected, Shares are derived

Fortress models NAS data as durable Datasets declared under `inventory/datasets/`, while Shares are disposable access surfaces derived from VM Mount and Service consumption declarations. Ordinary Datasets are adopted and protected: fortress validates their existence and owner UID/GID, but does not create, delete, or repair them by default; only Ephemeral Datasets created for Acceptance Tests may be destroyed. NAS Reconcile is conceptually API-backed, but can start as a read-only plan that compares declared intent with TrueNAS reality before write support is enabled. This keeps data ownership separate from access intent, lets unused Shares be reconciled away safely, and avoids embedding consumer-specific access rules inside Dataset declarations.
